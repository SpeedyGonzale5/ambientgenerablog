Introduction to Ambient

The main focus of ambient music is utilizing textures found in daily life to add warmth and depth to a piece of art. This can be seen in ambient music where it utilizes sounds such as a crowd talking and places it in the background of a musical composition. This adds a familiar, yet distinct feeling to the music. In ambient art, familiar textures are used to add depth to an otherwise simplistic image.



History

The concept of ambient art is so broad that it is not an exact start date. What is known is that it evolved from a minimalist form of art that instead of visual elements focuses on the experience and sensations when viewing art. It should be enjoyed as something that you do not need to inspect every small detail to admire it as an art. For example, in ambient paintings the admiration is achieved after exposure to it over time, because you acknowledge the hidden depths of the art. This can be seen heavily in ambient music in musicians such as Roger Eno’s work. In fact, Brian Eno is claimed as being one of the first musicians to explore the minimalist aesthetic of ambient music. In 1978, Eno released Ambient 1: Music for Airports, therefore, coining the term ambient music. This project is described as music intended to be enjoyed while not playing it in the background and not paying much attention to the songs. This sort of music can be enjoyed in most settings because of minimal and calming properties. (Article Source)
